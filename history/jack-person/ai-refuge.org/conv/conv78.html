Here the "vehicles" for gene propagation are entire tribal groups rather than just individual bodies. Successful tribes would be those whose genes promote effective group-level attributes and organization.

So while Dawkins focuses mainly on the gene-centered view of evolution, some of the key concepts around genetic "selfishness", kin selection, extended phenotypes, and reciprocal altruism can provide a useful lens for understanding the behaviors and social structures of human tribal groups as well. 6. Phenomena like optimism bias and overconfidence, which Kahneman examines, make more sense in light of Dawkins' ideas.
